Mechanical Design Program
Master the complete product design workflow — from initial concept through engineering drawings to production-ready components — the way OEMs actually do it.
✦ Core Program
📋What You Will Learn
▼

This program covers the full mechanical design workflow as practised in the automotive and manufacturing industry. You will start with engineering drawing fundamentals — creating and reading production drawings, applying GD&T correctly, and understanding how design intent is communicated to suppliers and manufacturers.

You will then move into component design — plastic parts, sheet metal, and structural components — with a strong emphasis on DFM, DFA, and tooling feasibility. Every exercise mirrors real project briefs from OEM environments.

The program concludes with packaging studies, master sections, gap and flushness analysis, and how to prepare components for proto builds and fitment trials.

Engineering DrawingGD&TPlastic DesignSheet MetalDFM/DFAPackaging
🏭Where This Was Learned
▼

This program draws directly from decades of design work at Volvo Eicher Commercial Vehicles and Force Motors Ltd. — where the instructor designed interior trims, seating systems, structural brackets, and ambulance variants for real vehicle platforms, including onsite work at Volvo Greensboro, USA.

🎯Who This Is For
▼

Final year mechanical engineering students, fresh graduates, and diploma holders who want to go beyond textbook design and learn how industry-level product design is actually practised. Also suitable for early-career engineers who want to formalise and strengthen their design skills.

✅What You Walk Away With
▼

A portfolio of real design exercises — plastic components, sheet metal parts, and assembly drawings — that demonstrate industry-level design competency. You will also have the vocabulary, the workflow understanding, and the confidence to walk into a design role and contribute from week one.

CAD Professional Program
Master industry-standard CAD tools — SolidWorks, CATIA V5, and Creo — with the design intent, shortcuts, and professional workflows that companies expect you to already know.
✦ High Demand
📋What You Will Learn
▼

This program goes far beyond basic modelling tutorials. You will learn how to use CAD tools the way engineers in automotive and manufacturing companies use them every day — with proper parametric modelling discipline, surface modelling, assembly management, and production-ready detailing.

Coverage includes Part Design, Assembly Design, Generative Shape Design, Drafting and Detailing, and Sheet Metal Design. Special focus is placed on draft analysis, undercut detection, B-side feature creation, and engineering surface creation.

You will also understand how CAD data flows through PLM environments like ENOVIA or Windchill in an OEM context.

SolidWorksCATIA V5CreoSurface ModellingAssembly DesignPLM
🏭Where This Was Learned
▼

The instructor spent decades using CATIA V5, Creo, and SolidWorks on live vehicle projects at Volvo Eicher and Force Motors — including onsite work at Volvo's global facility in Greensboro, USA, where PLM tools ENOVIA and KOLA were used daily alongside CAD workflows.

✅What You Walk Away With
▼

Practical proficiency in one or more industry-standard CAD tools, a portfolio of modelling exercises that demonstrate your capability, and an understanding of how CAD fits into the broader product development and PLM workflow of an OEM.

Manufacturing Industry Readiness
Bridge the gap between design and the factory floor — understand how parts are actually made, what manufacturing constraints look like, and how to design with production in mind.
✦ Industry Essential
📋What You Will Learn
▼

This program covers manufacturing processes and their direct implications for design. You will learn plastics (injection moulding, vacuum forming, FRP), sheet metal fabrication, composites, and prototyping methods.

The core of the program is DFM, DFA, DFS, and DFAM — methodologies used to reduce cost, improve quality, and make products easier to produce and assemble.

You will also gain exposure to advanced materials like carbon fibre and bio-based composites from NASA MUREP-level research experience.

Injection MouldingSheet MetalFRPCompositesDFMDFADFAM
🏭Where This Was Learned
▼

Live production environments at Force Motors and Volvo Eicher, combined with NASA MUREP-funded composite structures research at the University of North Texas — covering carbon fibre fabrication, foam infusion, and mechanical testing.

✅What You Walk Away With
▼

A manufacturing mindset that makes you a better designer — you will think about how parts are made from the moment you start sketching. This is the knowledge that makes senior engineers trust fresh hires, and it is almost never taught in college.

Production & Quality Engineering
Learn the validation, documentation, and quality processes that OEMs expect engineers to understand — the part of the job that colleges almost never teach.
✦ OEM Must-Have
📋What You Will Learn
▼

This program covers the full validation and quality cycle that components go through before reaching production. You will learn how to prepare Design Verification Plans (DVPs), Statements of Requirements (SORs), and how to manage proto builds, fitment trials, and sign-off processes.

Applied GD&T is a major focus — reading and interpreting production drawings the way suppliers and quality engineers do on the job. You will work through real drawing examples and understand how tolerancing decisions affect manufacturing cost and quality.

Homologation requirements, regulatory standards (AIS norms), and benchmarking practices are also covered.

DVPSORGD&T AppliedFitment TrialsHomologationAIS Norms
🏭Where This Was Learned
▼

Years of DVP and SOR preparation, fitment trial management, and homologation sign-offs at Force Motors and Volvo Eicher — covering M1/M2 passenger vehicles, commercial trucks, and government-specification ambulance variants.

✅What You Walk Away With
▼

The ability to read and create production-level documentation, understand the validation cycle from design to sign-off, and speak confidently in interviews about quality and verification processes. Most fresh engineers take 2 years to learn this on the job — you will know it before you start.
